Agricultural Schemes in India

Explore the top 20 agricultural schemes available for farmers in India. These schemes help farmers access financial aid, technical support, and resources to improve productivity and sustainability in agriculture.

Top 20 Agricultural Schemes in India

1. Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY)

The Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY) is a crop insurance scheme aimed at providing financial support to farmers in case of crop failure due to natural calamities, pests, or diseases. The scheme covers all food crops, oilseeds, and annual commercial crops.

Benefits: Subsidized premium rates, coverage for crop loss, and timely compensation for insured farmers.

2. National Mission on Sustainable Agriculture (NMSA)

The National Mission on Sustainable Agriculture aims to promote sustainable agricultural practices that enhance soil health, improve water use efficiency, and support the adoption of organic farming techniques.

Benefits: Assistance for organic farming, water management, and conservation practices.

3. Soil Health Management (SHM) Scheme

The SHM scheme focuses on improving the health of the soil through the use of organic fertilizers, micro-nutrients, and better management practices. It encourages the use of soil health cards for monitoring and improving soil fertility.

Benefits: Free soil testing and recommendations for appropriate fertilizers, improving soil fertility, and boosting crop productivity.

4. Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana (PKVY)

The PKVY promotes organic farming by providing support for the conversion of traditional farms into organic farms. It helps farmers adopt organic farming techniques, which contribute to better soil health and increased yields.

Benefits: Financial assistance for organic certification, inputs, and training programs for farmers.

5. Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana (PMKSY)

PMKSY aims to provide irrigation coverage to every farm in India, with the goal of achieving "Har Khet Ko Pani" (water for every farm). It promotes efficient water use and modern irrigation systems like drip and sprinkler irrigation.

Benefits: Irrigation infrastructure development, water conservation, and efficient resource management.

6. Rashtriya Krishi Vikas Yojana (RKVY)

RKVY provides funding to states for agricultural and rural development projects. It supports the creation of infrastructure like cold storage, market linkages, and post-harvest management facilities.

Benefits: Financial assistance for infrastructure projects, strengthening market access, and boosting agricultural productivity.

7. Kisan Credit Card (KCC)

The Kisan Credit Card scheme provides easy access to credit for farmers at subsidized interest rates. It is aimed at helping farmers meet their short-term agricultural needs, including the purchase of seeds, fertilizers, and pesticides.

Benefits: Easy access to credit, low interest rates, and extended repayment period.

8. National Food Security Mission (NFSM)

The NFSM aims to increase food grain production through area expansion, productivity improvement, and adoption of innovative farming techniques. It focuses on the promotion of high-yielding varieties of food crops.

Benefits: Financial assistance for increasing food grain production and promoting new farming techniques.

9. Pradhan Mantri Annadata Aay SanraksHan Abhiyan (PM-AASHA)

PM-AASHA supports farmers by ensuring fair prices for their crops. The scheme focuses on providing price support and ensuring farmers' income security, particularly during times of distress.

Benefits: Ensures minimum support prices for crops, helping farmers secure better earnings.

10. Neeranchal National Watershed Project

The Neeranchal National Watershed Project aims to improve the efficiency of water usage in agriculture by promoting the implementation of watershed management techniques. This project helps conserve water and ensures sustainable farming practices.

Benefits: Efficient water use, improved irrigation systems, and enhanced water conservation efforts.

11. Pradhan Mantri Ujjwala Yojana (PMUY)

PMUY provides LPG (liquefied petroleum gas) connections to women from Below Poverty Line (BPL) families. This scheme aims to reduce dependence on traditional cooking fuels, improving health and saving time for rural women.

Benefits: Free LPG connections, improved health, and reduced deforestation from cooking with firewood.

12. National Agriculture Market (e-NAM)

The e-NAM is a digital platform that connects agricultural markets across India, enabling farmers to sell their produce directly to buyers, minimizing middlemen, and ensuring fair prices for farmers.

Benefits: Better market access, transparent pricing, and improved income for farmers.

13. Horticulture Mission for North East and Himalayan States (HMNEH)

The HMNEH aims to promote horticulture in the northeastern and Himalayan states of India. The scheme provides financial support for the production of fruits, vegetables, flowers, and spices.

Benefits: Financial assistance for enhancing horticultural production and improving farmers' livelihoods in hilly areas.

14. Agricultural Export Policy (AEP)

The AEP aims to increase India's agricultural exports by promoting the production of export-quality crops and improving infrastructure for transportation and storage.

Benefits: Enhanced agricultural exports, market expansion, and better income for farmers.

15. Atal Mission for Rejuvenation and Urban Transformation (AMRUT)

AMRUT focuses on the rejuvenation of urban areas through sustainable infrastructure. The scheme ensures better access to clean water, sanitation, and better agricultural practices in urban areas.

Benefits: Improved urban infrastructure, better water management, and enhanced living standards.

16. National Agricultural Insurance Scheme (NAIS)

The NAIS provides insurance coverage to farmers against crop loss due to natural calamities, pests, and diseases. The scheme helps farmers recover their losses and stabilize their incomes.

Benefits: Financial assistance for farmers affected by crop damage, providing a safety net during emergencies.

17. Mission for Integrated Development of Horticulture (MIDH)

The MIDH focuses on promoting the growth of horticulture in India by providing financial assistance to farmers for the cultivation of fruits, vegetables, and flowers. It also supports research and development in the horticultural sector.

Benefits: Financial aid for horticulture production, boosting exports, and improving farmers' incomes.

18. Dairy Entrepreneurship Development Scheme (DEDS)

DEDS aims to promote entrepreneurship in the dairy sector by providing financial support for the establishment of dairy farms, processing units, and cold storage facilities.

Benefits: Subsidized loans for dairy farming, creating more job opportunities in rural areas.

19. Technology Mission on Coconut (TMOC)

The TMOC focuses on improving the productivity of coconut cultivation in India. The mission encourages the use of modern technologies for planting, irrigation, and disease management.

Benefits: Increased coconut production, improved quality, and enhanced farmers' livelihoods.

20. National Bee Board (NBB) Schemes

The NBB schemes encourage the promotion of beekeeping in India. Beekeeping plays a vital role in enhancing crop yields through pollination and supports the production of honey, wax, and other bee products.

Benefits: Financial support for beekeeping, training for farmers, and increasing honey production.
